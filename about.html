Our History

Six Decades of Excellence

BIL — Bilim Innovation Lyceum was founded in 1962 by a group of pioneering educators who believed that every child deserved access to transformative, world-class education. Starting with just 86 students and 12 teachers in a single redbrick building, the school has grown into a thriving community of over 1,800 learners.

Through the decades, Elmswood has continually evolved — expanding its campus, enriching its curriculum, and embracing new technologies — while remaining anchored to the founding values of integrity, inquiry, and compassion.

Today, our alumni network spans more than 60 countries, and our graduates occupy leadership positions in medicine, law, science, the arts, and public service across the globe.

1962

Foundation

School opens with 86 students and 12 staff on a 6-acre site donated by the Elmswood family.

1978

Secondary Wing Opens

The new secondary block doubles enrolment capacity; first cohort of A-level students graduates.

1994

Science & Technology Centre

State-of-the-art STEM facilities open, cementing the school's reputation for scientific achievement.

2008

Arts & Performing Centre

A 400-seat theatre and purpose-built art studios expand the school's vibrant creative programme.

2019

Innovation Hub

The new Innovation Hub opens with robotics, coding labs and a dedicated makers' workshop.

2025

IB Diploma Launch

Elmswood becomes an accredited International Baccalaureate World School.
